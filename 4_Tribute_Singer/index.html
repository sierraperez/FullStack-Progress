About Bad Bunny

Benito Antonio Martínez Ocasio, known professionally as Bad Bunny, was born on March 10, 1994, in Vega Baja, Puerto Rico. He is a Puerto Rican rapper, singer, songwriter, actor, record producer, and professional wrestler. He began his music career by uploading songs to SoundCloud, where his track “Diles” caught the attention of DJ Luian in 2016. His breakout hit “Soy Peor” established him as a leading voice in Latin trap. He gained international fame through collaborations like “I Like It” with Cardi B and J Balvin, and “Mía” with Drake.

Bad Bunny’s albums—X 100PRE, YHLQMDLG, El Último Tour del Mundo, and Un Verano Sin Ti—have broken records and topped global charts. His music blends reggaeton, Latin trap, hip-hop, and even rock and soul, making him one of the most versatile artists of his generation.

Outside of music, he’s also made appearances in WWE, including WrestleMania, and has won multiple awards, including Grammy Awards, Latin Grammys, and Billboard Music Awards
